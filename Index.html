Dubrovnik
Arrive 8pm · Check in
2 Nights

Arrive at Dubrovnik Airport at 8pm. Transfer to hotel, freshen up and head to the Old Town for a first evening glass of wine overlooking the Stradun. The UNESCO-walled city is magical at night.

🏨 Recommended Hotel
St. Joseph's Boutique Hotel
A 16th-century historic house fully restored within the UNESCO-protected Old Town walls. Just footsteps from the Stradun. Intimate, atmospheric and beautifully located.
Alt: Boutique Hotel Stari Grad — also inside the Old Town walls with a Michelin-recommended rooftop restaurant (Above 5).
🏛️ Things To Do
  • Walk the ancient City Walls (go at 8am)
  • Stradun promenade & old town exploration
  • Rector's Palace & Cathedral
  • Sponza Palace
  • Buža Bar — cliffside sunset drinks
🍽️ Restaurants
  • Restaurant 360° — fine dining on the walls
  • Nautika — classic Dalmatian seafood
  • Konoba Dubrava — local & unfussy
  • Buža Bar — sunset drinks on the cliffs

Mljet National Park
Day trip from Dubrovnik
Day Trip

Take the morning Jadrolinija ferry or Kapetan Luka Krilo catamaran from Dubrovnik to Mljet (~1.5 hrs). One of Croatia's most serene national parks — two interconnected saltwater lakes, a 12th-century monastery on a tiny island, pine-forest cycling, and crystalline swimming. Return to Dubrovnik for dinner.

⛴️
Dubrovnik → Pomena (Mljet)
Jadrolinija Route 832 or Kapetan Luka Krilo · ~1.5 hrs · Book at jadrolinija.hr
🌿 In the Park
  • Malo & Veliko jezero (saltwater lakes)
  • Boat to the monastery island (Sveta Marija)
  • Cycle the lakeside trails
  • Swimming in crystal-clear lake water
🍽️ Lunch
  • Monastery island restaurant — simple grilled fish and local wine with stunning views
  • Pack a picnic for the lakeside
🏞️ National Park 🚴 Cycling 🏊 Swimming ⛪ Monastery Island

Split
Full day exploration
1 Night

A full day in one of the most extraordinary cities in Europe. Diocletian's Palace is not just a ruin — people live, eat and drink inside its 1,700-year-old walls. Lose yourself in the marble alleyways, climb the bell tower, explore the basement halls.

🏛️ Must-See
  • Diocletian's Palace (basement halls + bell tower)
  • Cathedral of St. Domnius
  • Riva waterfront promenade
  • Marjan Hill hike (pine forest, panoramic views)
  • People's Square (Narodni trg)
🍽️ Restaurants
  • Zoi Split — long lunch on the Riva terrace
  • Bokeria — creative Croatian cuisine
  • Konoba Matejuška — local fish spot in the harbour
  • Uje Oil Bar — wine & olive oil cellar inside the palace

Vis Island
Croatia's most unspoiled island
2 Nights

Morning fast catamaran Split → Vis (via Bol & Hvar). Vis was a closed military zone until 1989 and remains gloriously off the beaten track. Filming location for Mamma Mia 2. Remote, characterful, extraordinary food and wine.

⛴️
Split → Bol → Hvar → Vis (Route 659)
NEW 2026 daily catamaran · Departs 07:30 · Arrives Vis 09:25 · ~€9–15pp · Book at jadrolinija.hr
🏨 Recommended Hotel
Pomâlo Inn
The most stylish hotel on Vis. Boho-chic boutique in a 1810 Venetian stone building, 10 rooms, bespoke linens, organic local products. Concierge arrange boat trips, vintage car tours. "Take it Easy" philosophy.
Alt: Hotel San Giorgio — old town location, gorgeous Mediterranean garden courtyard, on-site Boccadoro restaurant (one of the best on the island).
🌊 Activities
  • Blue Cave boat tour (Biševo island) — book early morning
  • Stiniva Cove — boat access only, stunning
  • Green Cave on Ravnik island — snorkelling inside
  • Komiža fishing village (taxi or scooter)
  • Vugava white wine tasting
🍽️ Restaurants
  • Pojoda — one of Croatia's best restaurants (book ahead)
  • Konoba Roki's (Milna) — legendary lamb under the peka, book well in advance
  • Boccadoro (Hotel San Giorgio) — superb Dalmatian
  • Try viška pogača — local anchovy pastry
🎬 Mamma Mia 2 Location 🔵 Blue Cave 🍷 Vugava Wine 🏖️ Stiniva Cove

Hvar Town
Croatia's sunniest island
2 Nights

Fast catamaran from Vis to Hvar (~40 mins). Hvar mixes history, lavender fields, glamour and some of Croatia's best food and nightlife. The oldest public theatre in Europe, a Venetian fortress, and the most beautiful harbour on the Adriatic.

⛴️
Vis → Hvar (Route 659)
~40 minutes · ~€9–12pp · Jadrolinija · Book at jadrolinija.hr
🏨 Recommended Hotel
Palace Elisabeth — Hvar Heritage Hotel
The oldest hotel on Hvar (1800s), named after Empress Elisabeth of Austria. Located directly between St. Stephen's Cathedral and Hvar's historic theatre on the main square. Staff greet guests from the ferry. Rooms overlook Hvar Bay or the Paklinski Islands. Breakfast included with made-to-order dishes.
Alt: Adriana Hvar Spa Hotel — strikingly stylish, rooftop pool and bar, prime harbour position. · Alt 2: Beach Bay Hvar — chic boutique, 30m from beach, sea-view balconies.
🌺 Activities
  • Hvar Old Town, St. Stephen's Square & Arsenal
  • Spanish Fortress (Fortica) — climb for panoramic views
  • Water taxi to Pakleni Islands — secluded coves
  • Bus to Stari Grad — UNESCO ancient Greek town
  • Hula Hula sunset beach bar (go by 5pm)
🍽️ Restaurants
  • Gariful — iconic Hvar waterfront seafood
  • Dalmatino — wine & Dalmatian small plates
  • Hula Hula Beach Bar — sunset cocktails
  • Mizarola — creative seasonal menu

Before You Go

Essential Tips

⛴️
Book Ferries Now
Route 659 (Split–Bol–Hvar–Vis) is brand new for 2026 and very popular. Book all legs on jadrolinija.hr as soon as possible. Tickets open now. You can also use the Jadrolinija app.
🏰
Dubrovnik Walls — Go Early
By 10am the walls are packed with cruise ship passengers. Go at 8am when they open — you'll practically have them to yourself. Pre-book online at visitdubrovnik.hr.
🔵
Blue Cave — Book via Concierge
The Blue Cave (Biševo island) fills up with tour boats by 9am in June. Book through your Vis hotel concierge for an early start. The cave glows most intensely around 11am–noon.
🍽️
Konoba Roki's on Vis
This legendary restaurant in Milna serves lamb cooked under the peka (traditional metal dome) — it requires advance notice of 24+ hours to prepare. Book ahead or you'll miss it. Widely considered one of the best meals in Croatia.
💶
Bring Cash for Smaller Islands
Vis especially has limited ATMs and some smaller restaurants and boat operators prefer cash. Bring euros from Split. Croatia uses the Euro (€) since 2023.
🌡️
June Weather
Expect 26–30°C, 10+ hours of sunshine, very little rain. The sea will be around 22°C — warm enough for swimming. Evenings are pleasant (18–22°C). Bring a light layer for after sunset on the water.
🏖️
Zlatni Rat — Arrive Early
Croatia's most famous beach gets very busy by 11am in June. Head down by 9am to claim a good spot near the tip of the spit. The beach changes shape with the current — enjoy the phenomenon.
🏞️
Krka National Park
Pre-book tickets at npkrka.hr and arrive by 9am. The park gets very crowded from 10am with tour groups. Drive from Split takes 1h 20min — head straight to Skradinski Buk entrance at Lozovac.
